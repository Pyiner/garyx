## Design Context

### Users

Garyx serves both technical users building and debugging agents and people who
use agents for writing, research, and daily work. They move between macOS for
dense management work and iPhone for conversation, review, and quick changes.
The product must be understandable without hiding the precision advanced users
need.

### Brand Personality

**Calm, capable, and exact.** Garyx should feel like a trustworthy instrument:
direct language, quiet surfaces, strong information hierarchy, and small
moments of warmth through agent identity. It is neither a playful AI toy nor an
enterprise control panel.

### Platform Direction

- Let each platform own foundational geometry and behavior. Use macOS-native
  structures on Mac and SwiftUI navigation, lists, forms, toolbars, sheets, and
  controls on iOS.
- Support the system appearance. Colors and materials must remain legible in
  light mode, dark mode, increased contrast, and reduced transparency.
- On iOS, take inspiration from the focus and progressive disclosure of
  high-quality conversation apps such as ChatGPT, without copying their visual
  skin. Garyx identity comes from agent presence, workflow semantics, concise
  copy, and restrained semantic status color.
- Use Liquid Glass or material for navigation and transient controls. Ordinary
  settings, lists, and reading surfaces use native content backgrounds.
- Avoid card-within-card layouts, decorative gradients, permanent shadows,
  custom input wells inside grouped rows, and icon-only actions when text is
  clearer.

### Design Principles

1. **The system owns geometry** — Start with platform containers and controls;
   customize only where Garyx has a distinct product need.
2. **Content establishes hierarchy** — Typography, grouping, disclosure, and
   concise copy should do more work than borders, fills, or ornament.
3. **One layer, one surface** — A grouped section is already a surface. Do not
   place every field inside another filled rectangle.
4. **Progressive disclosure over compression** — Keep common choices compact;
   give prompts, environment variables, and other complex content a vertical or
   focused editing space.
5. **Native, not generic** — Preserve familiar platform behavior while making
   agent identity, team relationships, runtime state, and workflow structure
   unmistakably Garyx.
6. **Color communicates** — Keep ordinary buttons, switches, and selection
   monochrome. Reserve green for genuine success, online, or running state and
   other semantic colors for warnings and errors.
7. **Motion explains change** — Animation should connect source and
   destination, expansion and collapse, or state transitions. It should not be
   decoration, and it must respect Reduce Motion.
8. **Accessibility is a layout input** — Dynamic Type, VoiceOver, contrast,
   reduced transparency, and 44-point targets are design constraints from the
   start.
9. **Consistency comes from behavior** — Shared navigation, save/cancel,
   validation, selection, and editing patterns matter more than forcing every
   screen into identical visual boxes.
10. **Quiet confidence** — Remove before adding. Every visible control should
    help the user understand state or complete a task.
